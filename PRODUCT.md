## Users

**1. Regular Customers:**
   - **Description:** Families, young professionals, remote workers, and students living near Whalley Range and surrounding areas of Manchester. They visit Bembe Cafe regularly, typically weekly or several times a week.
   - **Goals:** Quickly check the menu and prices, place an online order for collection or delivery, book a table for a casual meal or catch-up, and check opening hours before heading to the park. They already love the cafe and use the website as a consistent touchpoint.
   - **Frequency:** Regular (weekly/bi-weekly).

**2. Alexandra Park Visitors:**
   - **Description:** People who come to the park for leisure, parkrun, dog walking, or family outings and discover Bembe Cafe during their visit. They may not know the cafe exists beforehand.
   - **Goals:** Quickly understand what the cafe offers, see the menu and prices at a glance, find the exact location within the park, and decide whether to stop in spontaneously or plan a return visit. They need fast answers and strong visual appeal to make an immediate decision.
   - **Frequency:** Occasional at first, becoming weekly as they build the habit of stopping at the cafe during park visits.

**3. Halal-Conscious Muslim Families:**
   - **Description:** Families in Manchester who prioritize halal food options.
   - **Goals:** Need clear confirmation that everything on the menu is always halal before committing to a visit.
   - **Frequency:** As needed for dining out.

**4. Parents with Young Children:**
   - **Description:** Parents seeking a family-friendly cafe environment.
   - **Goals:** Check for child-friendly facilities, see the play space, find suitable food options for kids, and book a table.
   - **Frequency:** Weekly/bi-weekly.

**5. Remote Workers & Students:**
   - **Description:** Individuals looking for a cozy work-from-cafe environment.
   - **Goals:** Check wifi availability, atmosphere, opening hours before visiting, and see menu options for longer stays.
   - **Frequency:** As needed for work/study.

**6. Event Planners & Group Bookers:**
   - **Description:** Community group leaders, birthday brunch planners, crochet clubs, and social organizers needing a space for gatherings.
   - **Goals:** Table booking for groups, check capacity and special requests.
   - **Frequency:** Event-dependent.

**Shared Core Goals for All Users:**
   - **Fast tasks (under 30 seconds):** Checking opening hours, finding the address, seeing the menu, confirming halal status.
   - **Deep tasks (2-5 minutes):** Reading reviews and building trust before a first visit, exploring the full menu and special offerings.
   - **Overall:** Find information fast, feel confident the cafe is right for them, and take one clear action (ordering online, booking a table, getting directions).

**Emotions the site must evoke:**
- **Primary: Warmth** — Like walking into a cozy cafe on a cold Manchester morning where you feel instantly welcome and at home.
- **Secondary: Trust** — Especially for halal-conscious users and first-time visitors. The site should feel honest, transparent, and community-rooted, not corporate.
- **Tertiary: Delight** — Small moments of joy when browsing the menu. Beautiful food photography. Charming copy. A site that makes the user hungry and excited.
- **Supporting:**
  - **Belonging** — "This cafe is part of my community"
  - **Calm** — Easy to navigate, nothing overwhelming or rushed
  - **Pride** — Local Mancunians proud to have this gem in their park

## Product Purpose

Bembe Cafe aims to be a deeply embedded community hub within Alexandra Park, Manchester, offering halal, homemade food in a warm, inviting atmosphere. The website serves as a primary digital touchpoint to facilitate easy access to menus, online ordering, table bookings, and to reinforce the cafe's brand as a trusted, delightful, and inclusive local establishment. Success is measured by repeat visits, community engagement, and efficient service through online channels.

## Brand Personality

Warm, Rooted, Inviting.

**Warm:** The cafe feels like a person, not a corporation. Every interaction feels like it came from a human who genuinely cares.
**Rooted:** Deeply connected to its community, park, neighborhood, and values (Halal always, Homemade always, Local always). Proud of its specific identity and place.
**Inviting:** The design, copy, and photography should make every visitor feel welcome (child-friendly, accessible, open to various groups).

**Reference Sites:**
- Blank Street Coffee (blankstreetcoffee.com): Warm, minimal aesthetic, clean typography, community-first tone without feeling corporate.
- Grind London (grind.co.uk): Editorial food photography, bold but approachable design, coffee as a lifestyle.
- Ozone Coffee (ozonecoffee.co.uk): Earthy color palette, genuine community messaging, craft and care without being pretentious.
- Dusty Knuckle Bakery (thedustyknuckle.com): Warm, rough-around-the-edges authenticity, bold typography with a handmade feel, deep community identity.
- Ottolenghi (ottolenghi.co.uk): Presents homemade food as genuinely special and worth celebrating without being intimidating or exclusive.

**Overall Feel to Aim For:** Editorial warmth. Bold enough to stop scrolling. Warm enough to make users feel welcome. Honest enough to build immediate trust.

## Tone of Voice
- Warm and conversational — not formal.
- Community-first — not corporate.
- Quietly confident — not loud or salesy.
- Proud of being local, halal, and homemade.
- The copy should feel like a message from a friend who runs a cafe, not a brand manager.
- **Example:** Instead of: "We offer a wide variety of artisan beverages", Say: "Every cup is made with care, just the way you like it."
- **Example:** Instead of: "Halal certified establishment", Say: "Everything on our menu is halal — always, no exceptions."

## Anti-references

Bembe Cafe should NOT resemble:
- Generic food delivery platforms (Uber Eats, Deliveroo): Transactional, cluttered, purely speed-focused, lacking soul/warmth.
- Fast food chain websites (McDonald's, KFC): Loud, aggressive, optimized for impulse buying over community connection.
- Corporate hotel restaurant websites: Cold, overly formal, stiff photography, zero personality.
- Template-built Wix or Squarespace cafe sites: Default blocks, system fonts, generic colors, unintentional design.
- Dark, moody specialty coffee brands: Cold, unwelcoming, intimidating to families. Bembe is not a concept cafe for coffee snobs.
- Designs using purple gradients, glassmorphism, neon accents, or techy/digital/artificial trends.
- Stock photography of generic smiling models. All images must feel real, warm, specific to Bembe and Alexandra Park.
- Overwhelming homepages with popups, cookie banners, immediate newsletter modals, or autoplay videos with sound.
- Cold blues, stark whites, or harsh blacks in the design palette. The palette is specifically warm beige, olive green, and maroon.
- Designs that feel template-built; every section should feel custom-designed for this specific community cafe.

## Design Principles

1. **Authentic Warmth:** Prioritize genuine, human-centric design that evokes a sense of belonging and care, avoiding corporate or impersonal aesthetics.
2. **Community Roots:** Reflect the cafe's deep connection to its local community and values, showcasing its unique identity rather than generic trends.
3. **Intentional Invitation:** Craft visual and textual elements that actively welcome all users, ensuring accessibility and approachability for diverse groups.
4. **Clarity & Efficiency:** Design for rapid information retrieval and clear calls to action, respecting users' time and diverse motivations (e.g., quick order vs. leisurely browsing).
5. **Visual Delight & Trust:** Utilize high-quality, authentic photography and compelling copy to create desire and build confidence, especially around food and halal offerings.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Considerations for reduced motion, color blindness, and other accommodations. The cafe aims to be child-friendly and accessible. Emphasis on clear confirmation for halal-conscious visitors.
